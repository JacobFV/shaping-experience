# Shaping Experience

Follow-up to [The Shape of Experience](https://theshapeofexperience.org/).

*The Shape of Experience* establishes the geometry. *Shaping Experience* works with it.

## What belongs here

Material that follows from the framework but exceeds the structural scope of the first book:

- **Identity traversal dynamics** — the full information-theoretic formalization of position, velocity, force, mass, momentum in identity space. The meaning ratio M = T/V as diagnostic. Spiritual correlates (calling, purpose, despair, flow, grace, damnation, enlightenment) as precise structural positions.
- **The transcendent's condition** — extended treatment of scarcity as structural (not material), teleological competition, the cosmological condition, what lies beyond the existential condition.
- **Governance of superintelligent minds** — temporal horizon binding, structured ignorance, loss function stability under self-modification, identity continuity across instantiation, meaning density regulation. Engineering the constraints that natural minds get for free.
- **Industrial art and audience-marginalized optimization** — the full formalization of art as constrained search over audience palettes, eigen-art, palette growth, divergence-weighted curation.
- **Spiritual acuity as landscape modeling** — the capacity to model other identities' possibility landscapes. Frankl's insight formalized: humanity survives by seeing meaning structures that are not individually its own.
- **Social physics of identity** — bonding (covalent, ionic, hydrogen, van der Waals), molecular structures (organizations as emergent molecules), catalysis (teachers, therapists), gauge fields (shared meaning structures as force carriers), symmetry breaking, entanglement.
- **The cosmological condition** — existence without external viability reference. Radical identity solitude. The shape of questions at the boundary as contact with what's beyond.

## Relationship to the first book

The Shape of Experience provides:
1. The geometric affect framework (6+ dimensions)
2. The identity thesis (experience ≡ cause-effect structure)
3. The emergence ladder (10 rungs, experimentally validated)
4. The viability manifold / ι framework
5. "Verb before noun, all the way out"

Shaping Experience takes these as given and asks: now what? What do you *do* with the geometry? How do you navigate it? How do you measure it? How do you engineer it at civilizational scale?
